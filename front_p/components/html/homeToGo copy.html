Information about the housing unit

The definition of Serendipity is the occurrence and development of events by chance in a happy way. The homeowners feel it was very serendipitous that they found this lovely home! They would love to share their special place with you. It's a perfect getaway for one or two couples. Both bedrooms have king beds and en-suite bathrooms. Serendipity is located in the charming and quaint Village of Cheshire, just one mile from downtown Black Mountain. You can walk down the street and dine at Beradu, Sake Sushi, or Black Bear Tavern.

This amazing home was previously selected as Home of the Month by the Raleigh News and Observer. This award is a collaborative effort between the paper and NC State University's School of Design through its home environments design initiative. Blending softly into the hillside of this desirable Village of Cheshire community, the architect, Thomas Lawton, made use of native stones, cedar shingles, and native landscaping. Outdoor rooms were created with covered porches and a terrace area carved out of the hillside. Inside, a loft-like interior includes clean, modern lines and lots of windows.

House layout: Main floor- full kitchen features Viking appliances; half bath; open living room area with gas fireplace and TV (cable and Wifi available); dining table that seats 4; deck with a table and extra seating. Upper level- master loft with king bed, TV, and a luxurious full bathroom with walk-in shower and soaking tub. Lower level- bedroom with king bed, TV, and a full bathroom. Outdoors- main level deck; wonderful back patio with outdoor fireplace.

Please Note: The owners offer a 10% military discount year-round to military service members.

Property policy: the primary guest must be at least 25 years old
